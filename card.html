WOLF

Unlike other animals, wolves have a variety of distinctive facial expressions they use to communicate and maintain pack unity

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus
  • Average Length: females: 4.5 to 6 feet (tip of nose to tip of tail) males: 5 to 6.5 feet
  • Average Lifespan: up to 13 years in wild (usually 6 to 8 years) up to 16 years in captivity
  • Habitat: Some species of wolves only live in the United States and Others live in the cold regions of the Arctic Also, they live in Canada, Alaska and the northern United States, Europe and Asia

Wolves (Canis Lupus), are related to dogs, or more rightly, dogs are actually related to wolves. Wolves and dogs are quite similar in many ways, however, in most cases, wolves are larger animals. Wolves generally have longer legs than dogs.
